# Why I Almost Quit My Own Business
## 《我差点放弃了自己的生意》

**A branded microdrama for Claude (Anthropic) · v3.9**
**Runtime:** ~2:30 · **Format:** 9:16 vertical · **Locale:** Singapore (English + 中文)
**Architecture:** Coursiv-style 9-beat (HOOK → CONTEXT → JOLT → RECKONING → MENTOR → CONFESSION → TRANSFORM → PAYOFF → CTA)

---

## CAST

**GRACE TAN** — 28, Singaporean Chinese, slim athletic build, shoulder-length jet-black hair pulled into a loose low ponytail. Cream cotton tee, olive carpenter trousers, worn white sneakers. A delicate gold chain. A vintage Casio. Composed, gently tired, warm. Used to be a senior art director at a big agency. Quit eight months ago to start her own design studio. Audience POV.

**DANIEL LIM** — 28, Singaporean Chinese, Grace's partner. Lean wiry build, thin steel-rim round glasses, jet-black hair past his ears. Faded navy cotton shirt, charcoal pleated trousers. Calm, patient, the one who waits. Works in product management at a tech company.

**CARMEN WEE** — 30, Singaporean Chinese, the mentor. Sharp oval face, jet-black blunt bob, fitted black turtleneck under an oatmeal wool blazer, oxblood loafers. Runs a one-woman strategy consultancy out of Tiong Bahru. Unsmiling but warm — the look of someone who has been where you are.

**RAYMUS** *(referenced only, never seen)* — Carmen's own teacher. A creative director who taught her how to use Claude two years ago, before it was cool.

---

## SETTING

Tiong Bahru, Singapore. Grace's home studio in a 1950s walk-up flat. Carmen's regular café around the corner. The flat shared with Daniel. Golden hour, monsoon-season light through bamboo blinds. Cream walls, monstera plants, terrazzo flooring. Editorial documentary palette: oatmeal, cream, olive, charcoal, navy, oxblood. Kodak Portra 400 color science. Natural light only.

---

## VIDEO GLOBAL

Shot with Arri 35.
No music in scenes 1–7 (only ambient — keyboard clicks, kettle, traffic, kitchen sounds). Soft piano enters in Beat 8 ("Just one"). Returns in Beat 9 under the multi-Grace visual.
Dialogue: Singaporean English with light Singlish, plus Mandarin Chinese subtitles. Voice-over also delivered in both languages — English on screen, Mandarin Chinese subs below.
Documentary editorial photography aesthetic. Natural skin texture, visible pores, no airbrushing, no movie-poster polish. Subtle film grain. Muted, desaturated palette. Raw editorial honesty over glamour.

# BEAT 1 — HOOK (0:00–0:12)

**INT. GRACE'S STUDIO — GOLDEN HOUR — DAY**

Wide shot. Grace's home studio in Tiong Bahru. Cream walls. Monstera. Wooden desk with an iPad, a half-drunk coffee, a stack of receipts, an open MacBook, two phones charging.

A composite shot — seven Graces in the same studio, none looking at each other. Grace 1 sketching on the iPad. Grace 2 on a sales call by the window. Grace 3 at the kitchen counter writing a newsletter. Grace 4 reviewing a spreadsheet, brow furrowed. Grace 5 packing a courier envelope. Grace 6 on the floor stretching, neck cracked. Grace 7 staring at the ceiling.

The seven Graces fade until only one remains, sitting at the desk, head in her hands.

**GRACE (V.O.):** All seven of me are tired.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 我这七个人 —— 都累了。

CUT TO:

CU on Grace's tear-blurred face, the studio out of focus behind her. She's not crying loud. She's just done.

**GRACE (V.O.):** I quit my job eight months ago. I thought running my own thing meant I'd finally get to do the work I love.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 我八个月前辞职。以为自己当老板 —— 就可以专心做自己喜欢的工作。

# BEAT 2 — CONTEXT (0:12–0:25)

**MONTAGE — GRACE'S WEEK**

Quick cuts, all in one space:

Grace at the iPad — designing a brand identity at 11 PM. The screen reads "deadline: tomorrow 9am." Coffee #4.

Grace on a Zoom call — pitching to a new client. She's smiling, gesturing, the camera shows her from the chest up; the camera pulls back to reveal she's still in pyjama bottoms.

Grace at the dining table — laptop open to a spreadsheet titled "Q1 INVOICES & GST." Her face goes blank.

Grace on her phone — typing a LinkedIn post about a project she finished last month. She deletes it. Rewrites. Deletes it again.

Grace walking to the post office — courier package under one arm, two coffee cups balanced in the other hand, talking on the phone to a printer.

**GRACE (V.O.):** Turns out — designing is one job. Pitching is another. Sales. Accounts. Personal brand. Admin. Errands. Some days the actual designing — the thing I love — happens after midnight.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 但是啊 —— 做设计是一份工。Pitch 客户是另一份。销售。报账。个人品牌。Admin。买东西。有时候真正做设计 —— 我最喜欢的那一份 —— 要到半夜两点才轮得到。

# BEAT 3 — JOLT 1 (0:25–0:40)

**INT. GRACE'S STUDIO — NIGHT**

CU. Grace's MacBook screen. A client email is open:

> Hi Grace, we love what you've shown but the budget has dropped. Can we get the second round of concepts and the activation plan by Friday? Same fee.

Grace's hand reaches for the mouse. Pauses. Withdraws.

CUT TO: Wide. Grace sits back in her chair. Stares.

**GRACE (V.O.):** A senior art director at a big agency had three creatives, two account managers, a strategist, a producer, and a finance team behind every project.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 以前在大公司做 senior art director —— 每一个项目背后都有三个设计师，两个 account，一个 strategist，一个 producer，还有 finance team。

**GRACE (V.O.):** Now it's just me. And I can't afford to hire anyone. Not yet.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 现在啊 —— 只剩我一个人。请人 —— 我也请不起。还不行。

Grace closes the MacBook. Slowly.

# BEAT 4 — RECKONING (0:40–0:55)

**INT. GRACE & DANIEL'S FLAT — NIGHT**

Small dining table. Daniel has plated nasi lemak for two. Grace sits across from him, not eating, staring at her phone.

**DANIEL:** Eh. Phone down. Eat.
**DANIEL：** 喂。手机放下。吃饭。

Grace doesn't move.

**DANIEL:** Grace. You haven't eaten today.
**DANIEL：** Grace —— 你今天一整天还没吃。

Grace puts the phone face-down. Looks up at him. The mask drops.

**GRACE:** Dan. I think I made a mistake.
**GRACE：** Dan —— 我觉得我做错了。

Daniel doesn't react. Doesn't interrupt. Just waits.

**GRACE:** Maybe I should go back to the agency.
**GRACE：** 也许我应该回大公司。

Beat. Daniel reaches across the table. Doesn't take her hand. Just rests his palm flat near hers.

**DANIEL:** Or — maybe you talk to Carmen first.
**DANIEL：** 还是 —— 你先去找 Carmen 聊一聊？

# BEAT 5 — MENTOR APPEARS (0:55–1:10)

**INT. TIONG BAHRU CAFÉ — DAY**

Soft golden light through the window. Pendant lights. Bookshelf in the background. Terrazzo floor.

Carmen Wee. 30. Sharp face, jet-black bob, oatmeal wool blazer over a fitted black turtleneck. A flat white between her palms. The look of someone who has been where Grace is and is paying attention.

Grace sits across from her. Same cream tee. Eyes red from not sleeping.

**CARMEN:** So. You're doing seven jobs.
**CARMEN：** 所以 —— 你一个人在做七份工。

**GRACE:** I'm doing seven jobs. Badly.
**GRACE：** 我一个人在做七份工。每一份都做得很烂。

Carmen nods. Slow.

**CARMEN:** I did seven jobs for two years before someone showed me how to stop.
**CARMEN：** 我也做了两年的七份工 —— 然后有人教我怎么停下来。

# BEAT 6 — CONFESSION (1:10–1:30)

**Same café. Push in slightly on Carmen.**

**CARMEN:** I didn't think it would work for what I do. I'm a strategist. The thinking IS the work. I thought — if I outsource the thinking, what's left of me?
**CARMEN：** 我以前也以为 —— 这个东西对我没用。我做策略的，思考就是我的工作。如果思考都外包出去 —— 那剩下的我 —— 还是我吗？

**CARMEN:** Then a guy I used to work with — Raymus — showed me how he was using Claude. Not for spreadsheets. For the actual creative work.
**CARMEN：** 后来啊 —— 我以前一个合作过的人，Raymus —— 给我看他怎么用 Claude。不是用来做表格。是用来 —— 做真正的创意工作。

CUT TO: A montage of Carmen's screen — she's typing into Claude. The prompts visible:

> "Draft a brand positioning framework for a sustainable seafood startup..."

> "I have three concept territories — challenge each of them like a skeptical CMO..."

> "Write a 600-word LinkedIn post about why most rebrands fail. My voice is dry and direct..."

**CARMEN (V.O.):** First drafts of proposals — Claude. Concept brainstorming when I'm stuck — Claude. My newsletter, my LinkedIn, the cold pitches I used to put off for three weeks — Claude helps me get the first version out. Then I make it mine.
**CARMEN（旁白）：** 写 proposal 的初稿 —— Claude。Brainstorm 不出来的时候 —— Claude。我的 newsletter，LinkedIn，那些我以前要拖三个礼拜才发的 cold pitch —— Claude 帮我先写一版。然后我再改成我自己的声音。

CUT BACK: Café. Carmen meets Grace's eyes.

**CARMEN:** It's not magic. It's a teammate that doesn't sleep. But you have to learn how to brief it. Like a junior creative.
**CARMEN：** 这个东西 —— 不是魔法。它就是一个不睡觉的队友。但是你得学怎么 brief 它。就像 brief 一个初级设计师一样。

**CARMEN:** Raymus taught me that. It took me a Sunday.
**CARMEN：** Raymus 教我的。我花了一个星期天就学会了。

Beat. Grace looks at her, the look of someone about to ask the question.

**GRACE:** Carmen — will you be my Raymus?
**GRACE：** Carmen —— 你可以做我的 Raymus 吗？

Carmen smiles. Small. Real.

**CARMEN:** I was waiting for you to ask.
**CARMEN：** 我就等你问我这句话。

# BEAT 7 — TRANSFORMATION (1:30–2:00)

**INT. GRACE'S STUDIO — WEEKS LATER — DAY**

The studio is the same. The light is the same. But Grace is different. Hair up. Loose linen shirt. Calm.

CU. Grace's screen — Claude. She's drafting a project proposal:

> "I'm pitching a brand identity for a Singapore-based women-led coffee startup. Help me draft the strategic foundation first, then three creative territories. My voice: warm, direct, no jargon."

Claude responds. Grace reads. Adjusts the prompt. Reads again. Edits the output. Pastes into Pages. The actual rewrite takes her 20 minutes instead of two days.

CUT TO: Grace on a Zoom call. Pitching. The on-screen tile shows her relaxed, smiling. She's wearing actual clothes this time.

CUT TO: Grace at the dining table. Spreadsheet open. She's running a Claude prompt:

> "Look at these Q1 invoices. Flag anything that looks like a duplicate or a missing GST line. Be ruthless."

CUT TO: Grace writing her newsletter. Her draft is open. She's not writing it from scratch. She's editing Claude's first pass — making it sharper, funnier, more her.

CUT TO: Grace at the iPad. Designing. Just designing. The thing she actually loves. The clock on the wall reads 3:42 PM. Daylight. Not midnight.

**GRACE (V.O.):** I'm still one person. I still do all seven jobs.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 我还是一个人。这七份工 —— 还是我一个人做。

**GRACE (V.O.):** But now six of them get done in the morning. So I can spend the afternoon being a designer again.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 但是现在啊 —— 六份工早上就做完了。下午 —— 我可以重新做回一个设计师。

# BEAT 8 — PAYOFF (2:00–2:15)

**INT. GRACE & DANIEL'S FLAT — EVENING**

Same small dining table. Daniel has plated nasi lemak for two — again. This time Grace is already at the table when he sits down. Soft piano enters under the scene.

**DANIEL:** How many of you ate today?
**DANIEL：** 你今天 —— 几个 Grace 有吃东西？

Grace looks up. Small smile.

**GRACE:** Just one.
**GRACE：** 就一个。

She picks up her chopsticks. Eats.

# BEAT 9 — CTA (2:15–2:38)

**INT. GRACE'S STUDIO — GOLDEN HOUR — DAY**

Wide of Grace's studio at golden hour. Warm window light. Plants. The space breathes.

Composite shot — five Graces in the same frame. Grace 1 sketching on iPad at the desk. Grace 2 on a sales call by the window, smiling, gesturing. Grace 3 at the kitchen counter writing her newsletter on her laptop. Grace 4 reviewing a spreadsheet, calm, coffee in hand. Grace 5 on the floor with Daniel's dog, just breathing. They don't notice each other. They just are.

Soft piano under.

**GRACE (V.O.):** Used to be — if you wanted to run your own thing in Singapore, you needed a team of seven.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 以前啊 —— 在新加坡想自己干 —— 要养七个人。

Slow push toward the Grace at the desk — the designer one. She looks up at the others briefly, almost amused. The others fade gently, one by one, until only she remains.

**GRACE (V.O.):** Now the team fits on a laptop.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 现在啊 —— 一台手提电脑就够了。

CU on Grace's hands resting on her keyboard. Not typing. Just there. A small, private smile.

**GRACE (V.O.):** The future of work here feels lighter. Not for the big guys — they were already fine.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 这里的未来 —— 感觉轻了一点。不是对那些大公司 —— 他们本来就没事。

Pull back to the wide studio shot. Empty now. Just Grace, the light, the plants.

**GRACE (V.O.):** For the rest of us. The ones who used to have to be seven people. We finally get to be one.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 是对我们这些 —— 以前一个人要当七个人用的小家伙。我们终于可以 —— 做回一个人。

Title card fades in over the room:

**For the ones building alone.**
**Claude**

**给一个人在打拼的你**
**Claude**

Fade to black.
