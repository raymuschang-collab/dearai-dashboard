# Why I Almost Quit My Own Business
## 《我差点放弃了自己的生意》

**A branded microdrama for Claude (Anthropic) · v4.1 — LOCKED**
**Runtime:** ~2:30 · **Format:** 9:16 vertical · **Locale:** Singapore (English + 中文)
**Architecture:** Coursiv 8-beat (HOOK → CONTEXT → WOUND/RECKONING → MENTOR → CONFESSION → TEACHING → TRANSFORM → PAYOFF → CTA)

---

## CAST

**GRACE TAN** — 28, Singaporean Chinese, slim athletic build, shoulder-length jet-black hair pulled into a loose low ponytail. Cream cotton tee, olive carpenter trousers, worn white sneakers. A delicate gold chain. A vintage Casio. Composed, gently tired, warm. Used to be a senior art director at a big agency. Quit eight months ago to start her own design studio. Audience POV.

**DANIEL LIM** — 28, Singaporean Chinese, Grace's partner. Lean wiry build, thin steel-rim round glasses, jet-black hair past his ears. Faded navy cotton shirt, charcoal pleated trousers. Calm, patient, the one who waits. Works in product management at a tech company.

**CARMEN WEE** — 30, Singaporean Chinese, the mentor. Sharp oval face, jet-black blunt bob, fitted black turtleneck under an oatmeal wool blazer, oxblood loafers. Runs a one-woman strategy consultancy out of Tiong Bahru. Unsmiling but warm — the look of someone who has been where you are.

**CLIENT (V.O.)** — unseen client whose email opens the wound beat. Heard only as a notification readout.

**RAYMUS** *(referenced only, never seen)* — Carmen's coach, runs Dear.AI. The figure who taught Carmen how to use Claude one Saturday.

---

## SETTING

Tiong Bahru, Singapore. Grace's home studio in a modern walk-up flat. Carmen's regular café around the corner. The flat shared with Daniel. A morning MRT train carriage en route. Golden hour, monsoon-season light through bamboo blinds. Cream walls, monstera plants, terrazzo flooring. Editorial documentary palette: oatmeal, cream, olive, charcoal, navy, oxblood. Kodak Portra 400 color science. Natural light only.

---

## VIDEO GLOBAL

Shot with Arri Alexa, 35mm film, shallow depth of field.
No music in scenes 1–4 (only ambient — café chatter, kettle, traffic, train hum). Low piano note enters at the Henley email buzz in Beat 3 and sustains. Music swells under Beat 6 (Transformation) and Beat 7 (Payoff). Returns soft under Beat 8 (CTA), then pulls to silence on the final line.
Dialogue: Singaporean English with light Singlish, plus Mandarin Chinese. Subtitles below.
Documentary editorial photography aesthetic. Natural skin texture, visible pores, no airbrushing, no movie-poster polish. Subtle film grain. Muted, desaturated palette. Raw editorial honesty over glamour.

# BEAT 3 — WOUND & RECKONING (0:16–0:48)

**INT. GRACE'S HOME STUDIO — NIGHT**

Grace's home studio in a modern Tiong Bahru walk-up flat. Cream walls. Monstera plants. Wooden desk with an iPad on a stack of paper, an open MacBook, two phones charging. Bamboo blinds half-drawn, a single warm overhead lamp.

Grace at her desk, hair still up, sketching slowly on the iPad. Tired.

Daniel appears in the doorway holding two mugs of tea. Same faded navy shirt, glasses catching the lamp light.

**DANIEL:** Late one?
**DANIEL：** 又晚?

Daniel sets one tea on her desk. Sits at the corner of the desk. Doesn't push. Waits.

INSERT — Grace's iPhone face-up on the desk. The screen LIGHTS UP. An email notification slides down.

CU on the email preview text. It reads in monospace:

> Henley brief — sixty pages. Friday EOD.

Grace glances. Picks up the phone. Reads it.

**CLIENT (V.O.):** *(email tone)* "Henley brief — sixty pages. Friday EOD."
**客户（旁白）：** *(邮件语调)* "Henley 简报附上。六十页。星期五交。"

CU Grace's face. Something behind her eyes goes.

**GRACE:** *(reading)* Friday.
**GRACE：** *(读出来)* 星期五。

**[MUSIC: low piano note enters. Holds.]**

Daniel doesn't say anything for a beat. Then —

**DANIEL:** And Garrison?
**DANIEL：** 那 Garrison?

**GRACE:** Pitch Tuesday.
**GRACE：** 礼拜二要 pitch。

**DANIEL:** GST?
**DANIEL：** 报税?

**GRACE:** Wednesday.
**GRACE：** 礼拜三。

**DANIEL:** Newsletter?
**DANIEL：** 你的 newsletter?

**GRACE:** Three weeks overdue.
**GRACE：** 晚了三个礼拜。

**DANIEL:** And grandma's Sunday.
**DANIEL：** 阿嬷礼拜天。

**GRACE:** *(quiet)* I know.
**GRACE：** *(轻声)* 我知道。

**GRACE:** *(looking out the window)* I left the big agency to do my own thing. Every one of those is a full-time job. There's one of me.
**GRACE：** *(望向窗外)* 我离开大公司是想自己做。每一件都是一份全职工作。我只有一个人。

Daniel reaches across the desk. Doesn't take her hand. Just rests his palm flat near hers.

**DANIEL:** The version of you that quit the big agency — she didn't carry seven jobs.
**DANIEL：** 那个从大公司离开的人 —— 她没在扛七份工作。

**GRACE:** There's only one of me.
**GRACE：** 我只有一个人。

# BEAT 4 — MENTOR ARRIVAL (0:48–0:58)

**INT. MRT TRAIN — MORNING COMMUTE — DAY**

Inside an MRT carriage moving through Singapore. Pale concrete tunnel walls visible in fast horizontal streaks through the windows. Other commuters: a man in a polo shirt scrolling his phone, a student with earbuds, a woman in office wear reading. Each in their own world, each on their own phone.

Grace stands holding the vertical pole, one hand on the phone pressed to her ear. The carriage rocks gently. Cream tee, work bag on her shoulder, same dark circles.

The phone rings before we cut to her hand. Picks it up.

Phone speaker tinny — Carmen's voice slightly compressed.

**CARMEN (V.O. on phone):** You can do all of it. AND have Sunday.
**CARMEN（电话里）：** 你可以全部做完。还有礼拜天。

CU Grace's face, low voice.

**GRACE:** *(low voice, in the carriage)* How.
**GRACE：** *(低声,车厢里)* 怎么做。

**CARMEN (V.O.):** You're not gonna like it.
**CARMEN（电话里）：** 你不会喜欢这答案。

**GRACE:** Try me.
**GRACE：** 你说。

# BEAT 5 — PRODUCT CONFESSION (0:58–1:38)

**INT. TIONG BAHRU CAFÉ — LATER THAT DAY**

The same café from Beat 1. Different light — late afternoon. Carmen sits where Grace sat that morning. Grace sits across from her. Carmen's MacBook open, screen turned slightly toward Grace.

CU Carmen's MacBook screen. Claude's interface visible — clean chat window, purple Claude logo at top.

**[NO MUSIC — only café ambient]**

**CARMEN:** I tried Claude. Three things.
**CARMEN：** 我用 Claude。三种用法。

CU Grace, skeptical.

**GRACE:** *(skeptical)* The AI.
**GRACE：** *(怀疑)* 那个 AI。

CUT TO INSERT on Carmen's screen. A prompt is visible:

> Draft a brand positioning framework for Sea Group's new digital wallet sub-brand...

**CARMEN:** Sea Group deck. First draft in twelve minutes. Rewrote half. Real draft, not a blank page.
**CARMEN：** Sea Group 那个案子。初稿十二分钟。我重写了一半。是真的初稿,不是空白页。

CUT TO INSERT on Carmen's screen. New prompt:

> I have three concept territories for the Hoyo rebrand. Challenge each of them like a skeptical CMO...

**CARMEN:** Hoyo brief — new brand, no idea where to start. Asked it for five concept angles. Twenty minutes. They picked one.
**CARMEN：** Hoyo 那个新品牌 —— 完全不知道从哪里下手。要它给我五个切入点。二十分钟。他们选了其中一个。

CUT TO INSERT — Carmen scrolls. Newsletter draft. LinkedIn post draft. Cold-pitch email template.

**CARMEN:** Newsletter. Cold pitch emails. LinkedIn. Twenty minutes a week, all of it.
**CARMEN：** Newsletter。Cold pitch 邮件。LinkedIn。一个礼拜二十分钟,全部搞定。

CU Grace, listening.

**GRACE:** All of it.
**GRACE：** 全部。

**CARMEN:** *(leaning in)* But here's the part — it's not one assistant. It's many. All running at the same time.
**CARMEN：** *(往前坐了一点)* 这才是关键 —— 它不是一个助理。是好几个 —— 同时在跑。

CUT TO INSERT — Carmen opens three Claude chat windows side by side on the screen. Each one shows a different agent working: one drafting a positioning doc, one pulling competitive research, one writing a newsletter paragraph. Text streams down each window.

**CARMEN:** Last night while I slept, these three were running. One drafting Hoyo's positioning. One pulling competitive research for Sea Group. One drafting my newsletter.
**CARMEN：** 昨晚我睡觉的时候,这三个在跑。一个在写 Hoyo 的定位。一个在帮我研究 Sea Group 的竞品。一个在写我的 newsletter 草稿。

**CARMEN:** Woke up — three drafts on my desktop. I just edited.
**CARMEN：** 早上醒来 —— 三份初稿在桌面上。我就改。

CU Grace, eyes on the screen.

**GRACE:** *(watching the screen)* At the same time?
**GRACE：** *(看着屏幕)* 同时?

**CARMEN:** We're drinking coffee right now. One's still running — sorting Thursday's client brief for me.
**CARMEN：** 现在我们在喝咖啡。一个 agent 还在跑 —— 帮我整理礼拜四的客户简报。

**CARMEN:** *(beat)* All of it. I thought with it.
**CARMEN：** *(停顿)* 全部。我用它思考。

# BEAT 5.5 — THE TEACHING LAYER (1:38–1:52)

**SAME — CONTINUOUS**

**GRACE:** *(hesitating)* Carmen — I can't prompt. I can't code. I'm a designer.
**GRACE：** *(犹豫)* Carmen —— 我不会写 prompt。我也不会 code。我是设计师。

**CARMEN:** Neither could I.
**CARMEN：** 我以前也不会。

**CARMEN:** *(beat)* Later I got a coach. Raymus — he runs Dear.AI. He sat with me one Saturday. After that I could fly.
**CARMEN：** *(停顿)* 后来找了个 coach。Raymus,Dear.AI 那边的。他那个礼拜六陪我坐了一个下午。之后我才上手。

**CARMEN:** *(smiling)* Sunday. Two hours.
**CARMEN：** *(笑)* 礼拜天。两小时。

# BEAT 8 — CTA / FUTURE OF WORK (2:18–2:30)

**INT. GRACE'S HOME STUDIO — GOLDEN HOUR — DAY**

Wide of Grace's studio at golden hour. Warm window light. Plants. The space breathes.

Composite shot — five Graces in the same frame. Grace 1 sketching on the iPad at the desk. Grace 2 on a sales call by the window, smiling, gesturing. They don't notice each other. They just are.

**[MUSIC: soft piano continues underneath]**

**GRACE (V.O.):** Used to be — if you wanted to run your own thing in Singapore, you needed a team of seven.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 以前啊 —— 在新加坡想自己干 —— 要养七个人。

**GRACE (V.O.):** Now the team fits on a laptop.
**GRACE（旁白）：** 现在啊 —— 一台手提电脑就够了。

**GRACE（旁白）：** 这里的未来 —— 感觉轻了一点。不是对那些大公司 —— 他们本来就没事。

**GRACE（旁白）：** 是对我们这些 —— 以前一个人要当七个人用的小家伙。我们终于可以 —— 做回一个人。
