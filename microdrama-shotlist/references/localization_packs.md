# Localization Packs — SEA + Korea

Consistency packs for show localization. When the show is set in a specific market, apply the full pack — don't half-localize (Manila streets with Jakarta car brands is a dead giveaway).

## Jakarta, Indonesia

**Ride-hailing:** Gojek (primary), Grab (secondary)  
**Vehicles:**
- Economy: Toyota Avanza, Honda Mobilio, Daihatsu Xenia
- Executive: Toyota Fortuner, Mitsubishi Pajero Sport (signature elite convoy vehicle)
- Luxury: Lexus LX, BMW 5-Series
- Motorbikes: Honda Beat (masses), Vespa (hipster middle class)

**Fuel:** Pertamina (state-owned), Shell, BP  
**News outlets:** Kompas, Tempo, Detik, Jakarta Post (English-language), CNN Indonesia  
**Anti-corruption / investigation:** KPK (Komisi Pemberantasan Korupsi)  
**Streets & areas:**
- Financial/corporate: Sudirman, SCBD (Sudirman Central Business District), Mega Kuningan
- Old town: Kota Tua, Glodok (Chinatown)
- Middle-class residential: Grogol, Tanjung Duren, Pluit
- Kampung: Tebet, Menteng Dalam

**Religious/cultural props:**
- Muslim: tasbih (prayer beads), doa sticker on car dashboard, prayer rug
- Chinese-Indonesian: Buddhist altar with oranges, joss sticks
- Christian-Indonesian: rosary, cross pendant

**Currency:** Rupiah (Rp); amounts over a billion use "miliar" (Rp 70 miliar = 70 billion rupiah)  
**Brands for product placement:** Indofood, Telkomsel, Tokopedia, GoFood, Bank BCA, Bank Mandiri  
**Dialect:**
- Default: Jakarta Bahasa (casual, loose, Betawi-inflected)
- Chinese-Indonesian characters often code-switch with Mandarin under stress
- Elite/corporate: more formal Bahasa, English loanwords
- Kampung: heavier Betawi slang

## Manila, Philippines

**Ride-hailing:** Grab (primary), Angkas (motorcycle), Joyride (motorcycle)  
**Vehicles:**
- Economy: Toyota Vios, Toyota Wigo, Suzuki Dzire
- Executive: Toyota Fortuner (most common elite vehicle), Mitsubishi Montero Sport
- Luxury: Lexus, BMW, Ford Everest
- Motorbikes: Yamaha Mio, Honda Click

**Fuel:** Petron, Shell, Caltex  
**News outlets:** Philippine Daily Inquirer, ABS-CBN, GMA News, Rappler  
**Investigation:** NBI (National Bureau of Investigation), PNP (Philippine National Police)  
**Streets & areas:**
- Financial: Makati CBD (Ayala Avenue), BGC (Bonifacio Global City)
- Old/mass: Cubao, Quiapo, Binondo (Chinatown)
- Middle class: Mandaluyong, Pasig, Quezon City
- Slums: Tondo

**Religious/cultural props:**
- Catholic: Santo Niño icon, rosary, Black Nazarene print
- Domestic: ref magnets, Chinese horoscope chart (for Fil-Chinese families)

**Currency:** Peso (₱); amounts in million/billion follow English conventions  
**Brands for product placement:** Jollibee, SM, Ayala, Globe, PLDT, BDO, BPI, San Miguel  
**Dialect:**
- Default: Tagalog with heavy English code-switch (Taglish)
- Filipino-Chinese characters often use Hokkien at home
- Business: heavy English, Filipino surnames retained
- Regional: Cebuano, Ilocano for characters from province

## Seoul, South Korea

**Ride-hailing:** Kakao T (primary), Tada  
**Vehicles:**
- Economy: Hyundai Avante, Kia K3
- Executive: Hyundai Grandeur, Genesis G80
- Luxury: Genesis G90, Mercedes S-Class, BMW 7-Series (chaebol default)

**News outlets:** Chosun Ilbo, JoongAng Ilbo, Hankyoreh, KBS News, SBS News, MBC News  
**Prosecution/police:** 검찰 (Prosecution Office), 경찰 (Police)  
**Streets & areas:**
- Financial: Yeouido, Gangnam
- Historic: Jongno, Myeong-dong, Insadong
- Corporate HQs: Gangnam-gu, Seocho-gu
- Middle-class residential: Mapo, Yongsan

**Cultural props:**
- Convenience: CU, GS25, 7-Eleven (local versions)
- Food: soju bottles, makgeolli, banchan dishes
- Traditional: hanbok (seasonal), jesa altar

**Currency:** Won (₩); amounts use "억" (100 million) — "10억 원" = 1 billion won  
**Brands for product placement:** Samsung (phones, TVs), LG, Hyundai, Naver, Kakao, Starbucks Korea  
**Dialect:**
- Standard: Seoul Korean
- Honorific distinctions critical: 반말 (casual) vs 존댓말 (formal) — mark dialog with honorific level
- Business: heavy formal, frequent use of 부장 (buchang — "manager"), 대표 (daepyo — "CEO")
- Regional: Busan satoori for working-class/tough characters

## Cross-market consistency rules

1. **Pick one market per show.** No Jakarta show with Manila props "because it's SEA-set". Localize fully or don't localize.
2. **Brands align with setting.** Gojek + SCBD + Pajero Sport = Jakarta. Grab + Makati + Fortuner = Manila. Don't mix.
3. **Dialogue, names, and props must match.** A Jakarta show can have a character of Chinese descent who code-switches to Mandarin, but the brand placements must stay Jakarta.
4. **Currency amounts use local conventions.** Rp in miliar (Jakarta); ₱ in millions (Manila); ₩ in 억 (Seoul).
5. **Religious iconography is specific.** Tasbih = Muslim (Jakarta default). Santo Niño = Catholic (Manila default). Don't mix.
6. **Street names and districts are checkable.** Use real streets. A script set on "Sudirman" but describing "Makati traffic" is a red flag in the plausibility audit.

## Plausibility signals per market

When running the plausibility audit (in the scriptwriter skill), these are common red flags:

- **Jakarta:** using "taxi" generically when Gojek/GrabCar would be the actual choice; showing Christmas decorations in a Muslim-majority neighborhood; wrong police hierarchy (KPK vs. Polri confusion)
- **Manila:** showing snow; having characters drive themselves in Manila CBD during rush hour (unrealistic — they'd have drivers); calling a "sari-sari store" a "corner shop"
- **Seoul:** ignoring honorifics between senior/junior characters; showing wrong seasonal context; having characters use cash when Korea is almost fully cashless

Flag these in the plausibility audit and propose fixes.
