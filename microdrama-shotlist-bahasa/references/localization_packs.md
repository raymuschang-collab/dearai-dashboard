# Jakarta, Indonesia — Localization Pack

This skill is Jakarta-locked. All shows produced through this skill use the Jakarta pack consistently. No half-localizing — every prop, brand, dialect, and street must come from this pack. A Jakarta show with Manila props is a dead giveaway and gets flagged in the plausibility audit.

For other markets (Manila, Seoul, Bangkok, Ho Chi Minh City), use the generic `microdrama-shotlist` skill which has the multi-market localization packs.

## Ride-hailing

- **Primary:** Gojek (the dominant SEA-Indonesia super-app — rides, food, payments)
- **Secondary:** Grab (still common, especially in middle/upper segments)
- **Avoid:** Uber (left Indonesia in 2018), Bolt (no Indonesia presence)

## Vehicles

| Tier | Common models |
|---|---|
| Economy / mass | Toyota Avanza, Honda Mobilio, Daihatsu Xenia |
| Working ride-hail | Toyota Avanza, Honda Brio (Gojek/Grab cars) |
| Executive | Toyota Fortuner, Mitsubishi Pajero Sport (signature elite convoy vehicle) |
| Luxury / chaebol equivalent | Lexus LX, BMW 5-Series, Mercedes E-Class |
| Motorbikes | Honda Beat (mass), Yamaha NMAX (middle), Vespa (hipster middle class) |

The Pajero Sport is the visual shorthand for "elite criminal convoy" in Indonesian crime/thriller microdrama. Tinted windows, black-on-black, often parked across an alley as a threat.

## Fuel stations

- **Primary:** Pertamina (state-owned; 24-hour stations on Sudirman, Thamrin, Mega Kuningan)
- **Secondary:** Shell, BP

Pertamina canopies are visually distinctive — red and white, fluorescent-lit. Useful for night exteriors with character introspection.

## News outlets

- **Print/online:** Kompas (most authoritative), Tempo (investigative), Detik (fastest), Jakarta Post (English-language), CNN Indonesia
- **TV:** Metro TV, Kompas TV, TVOne
- **Investigative red flag:** if dialogue cites "Bloomberg" for an Indonesian local story, that's wrong — Kompas / Tempo / Detik are the right citations

## Investigation / law enforcement

- **KPK** (Komisi Pemberantasan Korupsi) — anti-corruption only; high-profile, often shown raiding offices in jaket biru
- **Polri** (Polisi Republik Indonesia) — general police force
- **Kejaksaan Agung** — Attorney General's Office
- Don't confuse KPK with regular police. KPK only handles corruption. Police vs. KPK confusion is a common authenticity fail.

## Streets, areas, districts

| Type | Names |
|---|---|
| Financial / corporate | Sudirman, SCBD (Sudirman Central Business District), Mega Kuningan, Thamrin |
| Embassies / govt | Menteng, Kuningan |
| Old town / Chinatown | Kota Tua, Glodok |
| Middle-class residential | Grogol, Tanjung Duren, Pluit, Kelapa Gading |
| Kampung (working-class urban village) | Tebet, Menteng Dalam, Cipinang |
| Trendy / nightlife | Kemang, SCBD, Senopati |

The contrast between Sudirman/SCBD (corporate glass towers) and kampung (cramped wooden houses, narrow gangs) is the canonical class-divide visual.

## Religious / cultural props

**Muslim characters (default majority):**
- Tasbih (prayer beads) — handheld, prayed with under stress
- Doa stickers / Quranic verses on car dashboards
- Prayer rug, often visible folded in a corner of a kost room
- Hijab on women characters who are practicing
- Mosque calls to prayer (azan) as ambient SFX five times daily

**Chinese-Indonesian characters:**
- Buddhist altar with oranges, joss sticks
- Red lanterns during Imlek (Chinese New Year)
- Often surnamed Wijaya, Tan, Lim, Sutanto
- Code-switch with Mandarin under stress (especially when stakes are family / financial)

**Christian-Indonesian characters:**
- Rosary, cross pendant
- Salib (cross) on dashboard
- Often Batak, Manadonese, or Ambonese ethnicity in the show's casting

## Currency

**Rupiah (Rp).**

Amount conventions:
- Hundreds of millions: `Rp 500 juta` (juta = million)
- Single digit billions: `Rp 7 miliar` (miliar = billion — common in corruption stories)
- Tens of billions: `Rp 70 miliar` (this is the "huge sum" range for the genre)
- Hundreds of billions: `Rp 700 miliar`
- Trillions: `Rp 1 triliun`

Avoid quoting amounts in the millions-of-dollars frame for Indonesian-set stories — it reads inauthentic. Convert to rupiah and use the local denomination.

## Dialect

**Default:** Jakarta Bahasa — casual, loose, Betawi-inflected. Sentence-final particles like `dong`, `sih`, `kok`, `lah`. First-person pronoun `gue` (very informal). Second-person `lo` (informal) or `Pak/Bu/Mas/Mbak` (respectful).

**Code-switch patterns:**
- Chinese-Indonesian characters often switch to Mandarin or Hokkien under emotional stress, especially with family
- Elite / corporate characters drop English loanwords ("meeting," "deadline," "stakeholder")
- Kampung characters lean heavier on Betawi slang (`enggak` → `kagak`, `tidak` → `nggak`)

**Honorifics:**
- `Pak` — sir, for older men
- `Bu` — ma'am, for older women
- `Mas` — for slightly-older men or service workers
- `Mbak` — for slightly-older women or service workers
- `Bro` / `Bos` — peer-level, casual

## Plausibility red flags (common authenticity fails)

When auditing a Bahasa shotlist, watch for these:

1. **Generic "taxi" or ride share** — the actual choice in Jakarta is Gojek (motorbike) or GrabCar / Gojek Car. A character "calling a taxi" reads dated.
2. **Wrong police/KPK confusion** — KPK does corruption, period. Don't have KPK officers handling a homicide. Don't have regular Polri arresting a high-profile politician without KPK involvement.
3. **Currency in too-small denominations** — "Rp 50 million" sounds like a lot in English but is only ~$3K USD. Plot stakes need to scale up to `miliar` (billion) for serious crime/thriller.
4. **Religious mismatch** — showing Christmas decorations in a Muslim-majority neighborhood (or vice versa) without a story reason is a dead giveaway.
5. **Wrong vehicle for class** — having a kampung character drive a Pajero Sport (or a corrupt politician driving an Avanza) breaks visual class hierarchy.
6. **English signage in non-English locations** — most Jakarta street signs, shop names, and government documents are in Bahasa. English signage shows up only in elite financial districts (SCBD, Mega Kuningan) and some Western F&B chains.
7. **Snow / autumn / winter weather** — Jakarta is tropical year-round. Heat, rain, traffic are the only weather modes.
8. **Westernized character dialogue** — characters using American slang ("dude," "for real," "no way") without code-switch context reads off. Use Jakarta slang patterns.
9. **Wrong day-of-week rhythms** — Friday afternoons are jam-packed (men leaving for Friday prayers), Sundays are family/mall time. Mid-week corporate is on Sudirman.

Flag any of these in the plausibility audit and propose a localized fix.

Pick Jakarta. Keep Jakarta. Every show this skill produces is set in Indonesia and uses this pack consistently. If a script needs Manila or Seoul, that's a different skill.
